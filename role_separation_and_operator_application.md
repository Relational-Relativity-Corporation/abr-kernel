# Role Separation and Operator Application

**Metatron Dynamics, Inc.** Reference data. Bounded over D. No claim beyond D.

Roles are not named into existence. They are the functions performed, and they reconstruct from the constraints that must hold for the workflow to function.

**Constraints.**

1. **No declaration, no evaluation** — there is nothing to evaluate before something is declared.
2. **Function defines role, not the label** — a role exists when its function is performed, not when the word appears.
3. **Description is not instantiation** — a document about a function is not the function.
4. **Responsibility stays with what has agency** — it cannot migrate to operators, documents, frameworks, or models, none of which have any. In the current implementation domain, this is the human.
5. **Verification detects divergence; it does not approve** — approval is downstream.
6. **Generation and verification require distinct frames** — a frame cannot detect the errors in its own blind spot.

**The roles, as they reconstruct.**

- **Origin** — declaration and responsibility: declares D, M, the relations and their provenance, and what is admissible. *Origin declares.*
- **Generator** — construction: proposes candidate structure within what Origin declared. *Generator proposes.*
- **Verifier** — divergence detection against declared criteria, from a frame distinct from the Generator. *Verifier observes.*

Origin is held by the human and is not assignable to a frame; the human cannot delegate out of it without vacating it.

**Application — the workflow.**

1. **Declare.** Origin declares D and M, the relations and their source, and checks admissibility: continuation declared everywhere, no relation fabricated. If none is admissible, stop — the operators are undefined, not approximate.
2. **Generate.** Apply A → B → R → E over the declared relations; apply any projection as a declared one.
3. **Verify.** A distinct frame detects divergence from the declared criteria and returns located findings — not approval.
4. **Revise.** Findings return to Origin; the constraints are refined; the pass repeats. Roles alternate across distinct frames between passes.

*The operators hold no agency; whatever the workflow commits to, a person committed it. Verification remains human. Bounded over D. No claim is made beyond D.*
